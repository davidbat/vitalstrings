About Heinz
The Heinz name is synonymous with ketchup, and is the most global of all US-based food companies. Beyond ketchup, other core products Heinz markets include sauces, meals, snacks and infant nutrition products. Heinz was founded in Sharpsburg, Pennsylvania in 1869 by entrepreneur Henry John Heinz. Now a $10.7 billion global company, Heinz products enjoy #1 or #2 market share in more than 50 countries. Heinz sells 650 million bottles of its iconic ketchup every year.
