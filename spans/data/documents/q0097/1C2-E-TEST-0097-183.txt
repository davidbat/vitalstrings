You can't spell
FedEx
without Federal Express Corporation, which does business as FedEx Express. The largest subsidiary of FedEx (about 60% of sales), FedEx Express is responsible for its parent's namesake express delivery business, the world's largest. FedEx Express delivers some 3.5 million packages per day throughout the US and to some 220 countries and territories worldwide. Packages (from envelopes to boxes weighing up to 150 pounds) account for most of the company's sales; FedEx Express also delivers pallets of freight weighing from 150 pounds to 2,200 pounds. The company operates a fleet of about 690 aircraft and about 50,000 motor vehicles and trailers.
