Order Number: PB2008-111483
Abstract:
On August 1, 2007 the Interstate 35W Mississippi River Bridge, in Minneapolis, Minnesota, collapsed into the river.  Thirteen people died in the collapse and 121 people were injured.  At the time of the collapse there were about 160 people on the bridge, carried by about 120 vehicles.  This report discusses the collapse, and the rescue of individuals from the water and their vehicles. Other issues described include fire and emergency medical services, recovery operations, the Minneapolis Emergency Management System, hazmat and environmental monitoring, and lessons learned and best practices.
