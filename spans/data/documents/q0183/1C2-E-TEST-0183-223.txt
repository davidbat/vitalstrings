Minnesota Flags to Half Staff in Honor of WWII Soldiers
Minnesota Governor Dayton has ordered all United States flags and Minnesota State flags lowered on to half-staff on all State buildings from sunrise to susnet on Saturday, June 25, 2011 in honor of Harry Lester and Ralph William Carlson. Lieutenant Harry Lester Bedard, a native son of the State of Minnesota, was killed in action on April 3, 1945, while serving as a Bombardier Navigator in the United States Army Air Force in World War II, and his family has been waiting sixty-six years for his return and proper memorial. Lieutenant Harry Lester Bedard’s remains were recovered in 1947, and identified by DNA in April, 2010. Sergeant Ralph William Carlson was killed in action on April 30, 1951 while serving as a tank driver in the United States Army during the Korean War. Sergeant Ralph William Carlson's remains were returned to the United States from North Korea sometime between the years of 1990-1994, and identified by DNA in February, 2011.
