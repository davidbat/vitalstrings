Biography
Marlon Brando, Jr. was an American actor who performed for over half a century and was perhaps best known for his roles as Stanley Kowalski in A Streetcar Named Desire (1951), Emiliano Zapata in Viva Zapata! (1952), Mark Antony in the MGM film adaptation of the Shakespeare play Julius Caesar (1953), for which he was nominated for an Academy Award, and Terry Malloy in On the Waterfront (1954). During the 1970s, he was most famous for his Academy Award-winning performance as Vito Corleone in Francis Ford Coppola's The Godfather (1972), also playing Colonel Walter Kurtz in another Coppola film, Apocalypse Now (1979).
Brando was the foremost example of the "method" acting style. He became notorious for his "mumbling" diction and exuding a raw animal magnetism and his mercurial performances were highly regarded. He is considered one of the greatest and most influential actors of the 20th century. Director Martin Scorsese said of him, "He is the marker. There's 'before Brando' and 'after Brando'. 
He was also an known activist, supporting many issues, notably the African-American Civil Rights Movement and various American Indian Movements.
