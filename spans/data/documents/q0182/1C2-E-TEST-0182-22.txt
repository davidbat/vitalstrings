The mystery of why we yawn may never be solved. Technically, a yawn is the reflex opening of the mouth followed by the deep inhalation and slow exhalation of oxygen. It is widely believed that we yawn when we are tired or bored but there is no evidence to support this belief. Yawning is an involuntary respiratory reflex. It allows for the regulation of carbon dioxide and oxygen that is being used .
by our bodies. Another great mystery is why we always seem to yawn in response to seeing someone else yawn. Scientists have been studying this for years but are no closer to an explanation.
