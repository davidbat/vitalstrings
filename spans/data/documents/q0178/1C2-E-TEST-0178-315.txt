When you look out on a sunny day, many of us just take a blue sky for granted, without wondering why it's that color. Oxygen and hydrogen atoms separate light into many different colors and scatter them. Because wavelengths of blue scatter better than the rest, the blue wavelengths show up and make the sky blue.
